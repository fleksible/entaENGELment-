## 0. Scope

This file defines the Assembly-Navi layer for tesser3TAKT. It describes how transitions are navigated through bounded spatial, acoustic, depth, pressure, and reentry roles.

The model uses source-bound role-forms already constrained by `docs/tesser3takt/SOURCE_DECORATION_MAP_v0_1.md`. Those role-forms remain controlled decorations or models; they are not proof material.

This file does not prove tesser3TAKT through physics, mathematics, music, biology, literature, or metaphysics. A motif may help name a transition role, but it must not become an identity claim about the architecture.

All motifs remain ANNEX-level model or decorative roles unless separately promoted through an explicit repository process.

## 1. Core Definition

[MODEL] tesser3TAKT is defined here as a transition-navigation architecture. It organizes movement between states through spatial, acoustic, depth, pressure, and reentry layers.

The spatial layer marks cuts, surfaces, cubes, slices, and projections. The acoustic layer marks tuning, resonance, carrier behavior, and readable modulation. The depth layer marks sediment, shadow, liminality, and non-walkable zones. The pressure layer marks membrane stress, overheating, refinement, and critical sensitivity. The reentry layer marks return, closure, leakage control, and answerability.

Canonical sentence:

"Raum wird geschnitten. Klang wird gestimmt. Tiefe wird durchstochen. Druck wird gelesen. Reentry haelt alles ehrlich."

## 2. Three-Cube Architecture

[MODEL] The Assembly-Navi uses three cubes as navigation roles, not as physical containers.

### Past cube

- enriched past
- sediment
- shadow
- V+
- not merely what happened, but what still weights the present

The past cube names the loaded background of a transition. It is the residue, memory, and weight that remain active when a present decision is made.

### Present cube

- membrane space
- decision space
- Whitehead/Bergson overlap
- where perception, selection, hesitation, and PASS decisions occur

The present cube is the inhabitable decision room. It holds perception and selection together without reducing hesitation to failure.

### Future cube

- stochastic possibility space
- path-fan
- not deterministic prophecy
- future as possibility halo, not oracle

The future cube names reachable or imaginable paths. It is used to keep possibility readable without turning possibility into prediction.

Guard: The future cube must not be read as prediction or prophecy.

## 9. PASS / HOLD / LOOP / STOP

[MODEL] Assembly-Navi decisions assign a transition state after guard and reentry checks.

### PASS

- transition is allowed
- guard and reentry are present

PASS means the transition can proceed because its role, pressure signal, slice logic, path cost, guard, and reentry are sufficiently named.

### HOLD

- keep in membrane / nectar / undecided state
- not enough closure yet

HOLD preserves the transition in an undecided membrane state. It is used when movement may still become valid, but closure is not yet secured.

### LOOP

- return for another winding
- useful when repetition is not identity

LOOP sends the transition through another winding. It is useful when repetition refines the path rather than merely duplicating it.

### STOP

- halt because overreach, leakage, false safety, or identity claim risk is detected

STOP prevents continuation when the transition would become unsafe, falsely complete, or identity-claiming.

A transition should PASS only if pressure signal, slice logic, path cost, guard, and reentry are all present.
